The Problem Nobody Talks About

Everyone Counts Parameters.
Nobody Reads the Equation.

When people talk about modern large language models (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini), they talk about scale. They obsess over billions of parameters, trillions of tokens, the "secret sauce" of RLHF, and the massive GPU clusters humming in the desert.

Almost nobody talks about the equation.

But every single one of these systems runs on the same fundamental update rule. This isn't just a clever neural network trick; it is a dynamical system. Specifically, it is a very primitive, very stubborn numerical integrator called Forward Euler. And it has been quietly shaping every drift, every hallucination, every confident wrong answer you've seen from a modern LLM.

If you want to understand why these models overcommit to wrong interpretations, double down instead of correcting themselves, and hallucinate with the absolute confidence of someone who has never been wrong in their life, you have to look at the math beneath the hood.

The Central Claim

We have built the most sophisticated thinking machines in history on top of the crudest numerical solver known to man. And the failure modes we call "hallucination," "drift," and "overconfidence" are not mysteries. They are mathematical theorems.

§ 1
Foundations

A Dynamical System Is Just Memory in Motion

Forget AI for a second. Think about a swinging pendulum, a cooling cup of coffee, or a car coasting on a highway. In each case, there's a state \( x \): position, temperature, velocity, and that state changes over time according to a rule.

Mathematically, a continuous dynamical system looks like this:

Continuous Dynamical System
$$\frac{dx}{dt} = f(x)$$

The state \( x(t) \) evolves smoothly. The function \( f \) is the "engine": it tells you exactly how the system changes at every nanosecond. The crucial word is smoothly. The system doesn't teleport. It doesn't make wild jumps. It flows.

Now think about memory. When you read a sentence, your brain doesn't just "store" words; it evolves an internal state of understanding. Evidence nudges your belief. Context dampens your doubts. A well-behaved memory system moves continuously. It doesn't leap from "I think it's a cat" to "IT IS DEFINITELY A NUCLEAR SUBMARINE" in a single step.

Intuition

A marble in a bowl is a stable dynamical system. Push it anywhere, it rolls back to center. The bowl's geometry enforces stability. The question is: does the Transformer have a bowl? Or does it have a ramp?

§ 2
The Integration Problem

When Computers Try to Simulate Continuous Reality

Here's the uncomfortable truth: computers are digital. They can't do "continuous." They have to approximate the smooth flow of a dynamical system by taking discrete steps.

The most naïve way to do this is called the Forward Euler Method. You look at where you are, check your derivative, and jump forward by a fixed step size \( h \):

Forward Euler Update
$$x_{k+1} = x_k + h \cdot f(x_k)$$

It's the first integration rule anyone learns. It's also the most dangerous one to use blindly.

When Euler Goes Rogue

Take a simple, perfectly stable continuous system: \( \frac{dx}{dt} = -5x \). This decays smoothly to zero. The analytical solution is \( x(t) = x_0 e^{-5t} \), pure exponential decay. Beautiful. Stable. Now apply Forward Euler with step size \( h = 1 \):

Euler Applied to Stable System (h = 1)
$$x_{k+1} = x_k + (1)(-5x_k) = -4x_k \implies x_k = (-4)^k x_0$$

Instead of decaying to zero, the system explodes, flipping sign and growing 4× every single step. A perfectly stable physical reality becomes chaos because the step was too big for the engine.

step 0x =  1.000  // initial state
step 1x = -4.000  // already flipped sign
step 2x = 16.000  // growing, wrong sign
step 3x = -64.000  // exploding
truth x = ~0.000  // what should actually happen

The Three Fates of Memory

The stability of the discrete map depends entirely on the growth factor \( \Phi = |1 + h\lambda| \). Three fates exist:

📉
Vanishing Memory
\(|\Phi| < 1\)

\(|1 + h\lambda| < 1\)
System forgets perturbations. Stable. Noise dies out.
📌
Perfect Persistence
\(|\Phi| = 1\)

\(\lambda = 0\)
State holds forever. Neither growing nor decaying.
💥
Exploding Memory
\(|\Phi| > 1\)

\(|1 + h\lambda| > 1\)
System amplifies noise until it destroys the signal.

For Euler to be stable, the step size \( h \) must be tiny relative to the system's dynamics. If you ignore this and fix \( h = 1 \), you are gambling with stability at every single step.

§ 3
Opening the Hood

The Transformer Is Forward Euler in Disguise

Now we open the hood. Strip away the marketing, and a single Transformer layer is defined by one equation, the Residual Connection:

Transformer Layer Update (GPT-4, GPT-5, Claude, Gemini...)
$$x_{k+1} = x_k + f_k(x_k)$$

Compare to Euler: \( x_{k+1} = x_k + h \cdot f(x_k) \). The Transformer is Forward Euler with step size permanently fixed at \( h = 1 \). Not 0.01. Not adaptive. One. Maximum force. Every layer. Every token. Every model.

Engine 1: The Matchmaker (Attention)

Inside \( f_k(x_k) \), the Attention mechanism does vector importance matching. Every token is projected into three spaces:

The Q, K, V Decomposition

Queries (Q): "What am I looking for?", e.g. the word "Mercury" searching for Space or Chemistry context.

Keys (K): "What do I contain?", e.g. "Orbit" carrying Space information.

Values (V): "What is my actual semantic content?", the raw data to be transferred.

Attention: Similarity Score → Weights → Output
$$\text{Attn}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)\!V$$

This is pure semantic importance matching. If "Orbit" gets weight 0.9, the model has decided it is the most important context. No fact-check. No stabilizer. No second opinion. Just a score, a softmax, and a jump.

Engine 2: The Accelerator (FFNN)

Once Attention gathers context, the Feed-Forward Network sharpens it. If Attention is the steering wheel, the FFNN is the gas pedal:

Feed-Forward Network
$$\text{FFN}(x) = \sigma(W_1 x + b_1)\,W_2 + b_2$$

If Attention says "I'm 51% sure this is about a planet," the FFNN amplifies that to "it's definitely a planet, push the state even harder in that direction." Then it hands that push directly to the Euler update at full force.

The Complete Update Equation

Full Transformer Layer: The Equation Under Every Modern LLM
$$x_{k+1} = x_k + \underbrace{\text{FFN}\!\left(\text{LN}\!\left(x_k + \text{Attn}(x_k)\right)\right)}_{f_k(x_k)}$$

The architecture is locked into \( h = 1 \). No adaptive step control. No stability check. The entire result of the matching-and-acceleration logic is injected into the memory stream at maximum force, every single layer.

"Nothing in this equation stops the system from taking a bias. There is no fact-check. There is no stabilizer. There is only the step."
§ 4
A Concrete Failure

The Mercury Problem: Hallucination Is Iterative Amplification

Imagine the prompt: "Explain why Mercury is important." Mercury is ambiguous: planet, element, Roman god, car brand. A stable memory system would hold this ambiguity open until evidence resolves it. The Transformer cannot do this.

Planet Bias Amplification Across Transformer Layers
L01
Neutral, tiny planet lean
+2% planet
L10
Attention finds "orbit," "solar"
+12% planet
L24
FFNN amplifies, "definitely space"
+35% planet
L48
Planet signal dominates, reinforces itself
+68% planet
L96
Convinced. Unstoppable.
+95% planet

At Layer 1, attention scores for planet-related tokens are 52% vs 48%, a tiny bias. That bias gets added to the residual stream at full force. At Layer 2, the hidden state is already planet-biased, so Query vectors naturally score planet-related Keys even higher. This is a positive feedback loop with no damping.

By Layer 80, the "planet" semantic direction has been amplified so many times it becomes an unstoppable signal. If you were asking about the element, the model overshot reality long before it generates your answer.

Hallucination is not random. It is iterative amplification with no friction. More precisely: if any eigenvalue \( \lambda \) of the Jacobian of \( f_k \) satisfies \( |1 + \lambda| > 1 \), that direction amplifies at every layer. LLMs are trained for prediction accuracy, not spectral stability, so these amplifying eigenvalues are everywhere.

§ 5
The Verdict

The Structural Flaws of a Fixed-Step World

Forward Euler is the junk food of numerical integration. Fast, cheap, parallelizable, which is why it's used. But the cost is structural:

Three Structural Flaws

Conditional Stability: High-magnitude transformations push eigenvalues outside the stability region. The model explodes in certain semantic directions.

Overshooting: \( h = 1 \) is maximum-force commitment at every layer. The model commits to an interpretation far earlier than it should, because it has no choice.

No Friction: Real stable systems have a damping term that pulls state back toward equilibrium. Transformers have no such term. They just pile nudge on top of nudge.

"We gave AI a Ferrari engine, fixed the steering to move in 1-meter discrete jumps, and removed the brakes. Everything begins here, with the fact that the most advanced AI on Earth never learned how to slow down."
